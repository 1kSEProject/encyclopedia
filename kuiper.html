THE KUIPER

OVERVIEW

Far on the outer rim of the system sits the Kuiper: a loose collection of dwarf planets and smaller objects that from a distance seem abandoned, but are far from it to the trained eye. The four dwarf planets of the Kuiper are Pluto, Eris, Makemake, and Haumea. They are connected by a series of gravity slingshots strategically placed throughout the Kuiper. The peoples of the Kuiper long ago foresaw the the Impact and the inevitable downfall of the Earth Empire. These elder humans fled to the furthest reaches of the solar system with countless books and records. It is because of their hardships the knowledge of the ancients is kept aflame.

PEOPLES

CULTURE

Few have seen a Kuiper in person, and this is intentional. Their appearance can be off-putting to one unfamiliar with their culture. Their frame is slender with elongated arms and legs that hold tight to the bone. Living in low-light has made their eyes round and their skin translucent so the shadows of organs wriggle beneath. This lack of physical prowess forced their culture to evolve. Intellect rules in Kuiper society, and it is more often an enemy is felled by structured argument than by point of sword.

Kuipers are born with a special gland in their palm, the product of genetic engineering, which when pressed expels a hallucinogenic vapor that induces a dreamlike state in an opponent exposing their subconscious. Many Kuipers duel with each other with this gift. Any outsider, however, will instantly fall under their control.

While Kuipers aren’t known for their strength, they are quite agile. Their homeworld, Pluto, has only 8% of earth’s gravity. Since the planet is covered in a protective layer of Plasma Kuipers are able to walk without suits. Children often exercise their legs by leaping off walls and up buildings (a 30-story fall causes at worst a minor bruise). They are surprisingly fast in low gravity environments, able to jump, drift, and land in complete silence.

GOVERNMENT

TECHNOCRACY

Kuiper governments are made up of highly skilled technocrats. Rulers are chosen in ceremony annually, the Machina Faire. Engineers, Scientists, and Mathematicians compete for power by proving depth of knowledge in their field. New inventions, theories, devices, and equations are shown off to a massive crowd of spectators and the top hundred are chosen to lead the Kuiper.

The winner of Machina Faire is crowned Virtuosi and is given the power to add, change, or repeal a single law. In this way the Virtuosi determines future path of Kuiper society.
