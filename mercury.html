MERCURY

OVERVIEW

Mercury is the smallest of the eight planets in our Solar System. Despite its close proximity to SOL it is covered in a sheet of ice. As a result of its rotation is subject to wild temperature fluctuations. Mercury was colonized in the year 850TE by two rival nations of proto-Earth. While the Impact destroyed any remaining evidence of who these nations were, we can see today that this bipolar divide still exists today between Mercury's two major cultures: the calculating Woshida and the steel-booted Drybones.

PEOPLES

CULTURES

DRYBONES

The self-styled Drybones rule the firey wastelands of the sun-facing side of Mercury. The planet's rotation forced the Drybones to build massive panel-covered wagons that move slowly across the planet collecting Sol energy. Drybones are quick to anger and quicker to drink. Their clothing is made of tanned leather from large genetically engineered Bulls which also provide transportation and food to the population.

WOSHIDA

Woshida rule the icey dark-side of Mercury. There are many smaller settlements, but by far the most populous city is the Shijie Gui - a massive mechanical turtle that slowly traverses the icy night in step with the planet. Due to the harshness of the dark-side environment, lack of food and Sol energy the Woshida are often forced sacrifice members of their population so their society may survive. This has given them a reputation of being cold and calculating to outsiders.
