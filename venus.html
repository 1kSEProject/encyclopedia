VENUS

OVERVIEW

The Building of a New World

It is hard to fathom when viewing the lush, color-full canopies of modern Venus that the planet was originally a molten rock of 462 degrees with a rotation so slow it was incapable of growing vegitation. The first attempt at terraforming the planet in 186SE was a disaster. Hundreds of trebuchets in the Belt flung asteroids at the surface in an attempt to speed the planets rotation closer to the typical 24 hour day/night cycle of Earth. Instead, the massive rocks nearly knocked the planet out of orbit and into the Sun. The scars of this debacle are still visible today as the massive canyons across the equator known as the Black Tear.

The second attempt at terraforming was much more successful. Particle-spinners placed in the upper atmosphere ejected thick clouds out into space at high velocity. This not only brought the planets spin to a manageable 15 Day / 15 Night cycle, but it had the side effect of thinning the atmosphere and lowering the surface temperature to a breezey 22 degrees. Soon after the botonists got to work, hyper-growing jungles across the surface whose soil was fresh and unspoiled. It wasn't long after that colonists started arriving in droves. Unfortunately, life in the jungle would prove to be a dangerous endeavor.

PEOPLES

CIVILIZATIONS

Overview

Kymera are most at home in the jungle cities of Venus. It is customary for Kymera to splice themselves with another animal for style and utility.

Kymeran civilization is typical of most terrestrial animals; two partners raise their young as a family. However, since their culture has evolved to be co-dependant with biotechnology there are a few differences of note. First, the gender of the parents do not matter for reproduction. Anyone may mix genes with another and have children. Second, the race has not yet perfected the use of biotech and so most children die soon after birth. This causes Kymera to breed frequently, and as a result it is common to see families with ten or more children.

At age twelve Kymera have a coming of age ceremony where they hunt down and kill an animal to bond with it. The village Shaman uses a crude form of surgery and relic-magics to merge the animal carcass with the hunter. Afterwards, depending on the animal chosen the young Kymera is sent to live in one of the Four Nations.

Major Cities

BUCKMINSTER

A floating city of hot air and woven nests. Blimps carry passengers to creaky docks. Vehicles travel in windy slipstreams.

ALGOR

A city of paper lanterns and wooden walkways in the branches near the canopy. This is the main hub for off-worlders.

OBERSHAAN

The creatures of the sea live in the hollowed out, water-full trunks of massive trees.
